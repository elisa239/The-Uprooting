THE UPROOTING

Voices from the
Refugee Crisis

The Uprooting is a multimedia documentary project that explores the refugee crisis through the life-stories of its protagonists. It aims to go beyond the daily headlines and forefront the voices of refugees themselves in an in-depth examination of the traumas of war, the challenges of exile and the contours of an evolving diaspora. The project consists two components. The first and most substantive section is a collection of interviews with a plurality of refugees in different stages of the prolonged and problematic process of migration—beginning with people making arrangements to be smuggled out of Turkey and ending with those who have been granted asylum in countries like Sweden, Switzerland, and Germany. The Uprooting’s second component displays the work of photographers who have been documenting the crisis in the different places where it continues to unfold.

The Uprooting aims to become an open platform so that different actors can make contributions. We hope that more and more refugees, journalists, and photographers add their own stories to this growing polyphonic narrative of mass migration.

The Uprooting website will be released this December 2015.
